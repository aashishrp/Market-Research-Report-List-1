<p><p>Electroless Copper for IC Substrates refers to the process of depositing a layer of copper onto the surfaces of integrated circuit substrates without the need for an external electrical source. The market for Electroless Copper can be categorized into two types: Horizontal Electroless Copper and Vertical Electroless Copper. Horizontal Electroless Copper deposition occurs in a flat, parallel manner, suitable for substrates with larger surface areas. Vertical Electroless Copper deposition, on the other hand, is a vertically oriented process, ideal for substrates with small, deep holes or trenches.</p></p>
